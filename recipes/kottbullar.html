Köttbullar

Meatballs - The National Dish of Sweden!

If you've ever been to an IKEA, you've doubtless ordered the köttbullar (pronounced like shöttbullar, but we don't correct people in public) and fallen in love. Often enjoyed with boiled potatoes, and as tasty as they are filling!

For 4-6 servings.

Ingredients

  • Some butter
  • 75g (3/4 cup) white breadcrumbs
  • 500g (18 oz) ground beef/pork mixture
  • 250ml (1 1/4 cup) milk
  • 1 egg
  • 1 onion
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Ground allspice

Steps

  1. Soak breadcrumbs in milk, ≈10 minutes.
  2. Dice onion and fry gently in butter without browning.
  3. Blend meat mixture with onion, egg, milk/breadcrumb mixture, and the spices.
  4. Add salt and pepper. Continue blending 5-10 minutes. Add a little water if too firm.
  5. Check the taste by test-frying one meatball.
  6. Shape the meatballs——easier if you butter your hands, or use two spoons——then place on clean plates.
  7. Brown a pat of butter in pan. Place meatballs in once it stops sizzling.
  8. Shake pan regularly and brown all sides. Continue until desired colour.

Thanks to Matthias Kamann. This recipe is in his book, How To Be Swedish.
